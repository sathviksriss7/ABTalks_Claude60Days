01 / About

From the plant floor
to the model card

I started as a process engineer, scaling chemical intermediates from a lab beaker to a 60x manufacturing run worth roughly $35M in product. That's where I learned that a system only matters once it survives scale — a lesson I carried straight into product management.

After an MBA at IIM Calcutta, I moved into product at EXL Service, where I now lead Generative AI strategy for healthcare clients — building the 12-month roadmap, running two agile teams, and shipping LLM-powered MVPs that have generated over $17M in revenue across four engagements. Along the way I've evaluated agents on accuracy and clinical relevance, cut R&D cloud spend by 28%, and pushed sprint velocity up 35% by getting AI coding assistants into the hands of developers who'd never used them.

What I'm doing now is narrowing the gap between "I manage AI products" and "I can build and evaluate the systems myself" — going deeper into Python, evals, and applied data science so the roadmaps I write are backed by hands-on technical judgment, not just stakeholder management.
