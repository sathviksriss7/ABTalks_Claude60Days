Reference

Nutrition sources & disclaimer

Where the reference values in NutriScope come from, and the limits of what this tool can tell you.

Data sources
ICMR–NIN Nutrient Requirements for Indians (RDA 2020)
Used as the basis for protein, fiber, iron, calcium, vitamin and mineral RDA targets adjusted by age and gender.
USDA FoodData Central
Reference point for approximate per-100g macro and micronutrient values for common foods used in the NutriScope database.
Mifflin–St Jeor Equation (1990)
Used to estimate Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) from age, gender, height and weight, then scaled by activity level to estimate Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE).
WHO / NIH Dietary Reference Guidance
Informs general thresholds used for sodium upper-limit guidance and macro distribution ranges.
Educational disclaimer

NutriScope is an educational self-tracking tool. All calculations — energy targets, micronutrient RDAs, deficiency/excess flags and risk analysis — are simplified estimates derived from population-level averages and approximate food composition data. They do not account for individual medical history, lab results, medications, allergies or specific health conditions. Nothing in this app constitutes medical advice, diagnosis or treatment. Always consult a registered dietitian, nutritionist or physician before making significant dietary changes, especially if you are pregnant, managing a chronic condition, or have a diagnosed nutrient deficiency. This app runs entirely in your browser — no data is transmitted or stored externally.
